Learn From Sti Alumni

Part 3

Staying Motivated and Engaged:

Maintaining motivation throughout one's academic journey is vital for success. The interviewee encouraged students to stay focused on their goals, recognizing that as IT students, they were planting the seeds of their future. Their analogy painted a vivid picture of growth and development. As they explained, the four years in college serve as a training ground, preparing students for the real challenges that come after graduation. Continuous learning and an unwavering focus on one's goals are key to thriving in the IT industry.

Most Exhausting Experience at STI College:

The graduate recalls their most exhausting experience, which involved the overwhelming convergence of tasks during finals. Juggling exams, a thesis, multimedia projects, and document revisions is not for the faint of heart. However, they view these challenges as valuable training for real-life situations. As students, they are honing their problem-solving skills, learning to adapt, and discovering the art of effective time management. These trials, they note, prepare them for the responsibilities and pressures they will encounter in their future careers.

Favorite College Campus Activities:

Amid the academic rigor, the graduate found moments of joy in campus events and extracurricular activities. They particularly enjoyed participating in these events as they provided a break from the demands of academia and allowed students to bond with classmates. It's a reminder that college life is not solely about textbooks and lectures; it's also about forming lasting connections, creating fun memories, and embracing the diverse experiences that a campus has to offer.

Memorable Moments from the First Year:

Memorable Moments from the First Year: The graduate fondly looks back on their first year, recalling the challenges of transitioning from a two-year degree program to a four-year bachelor's degree. They found themselves ahead of their peers in terms of experience, having already delved into the IT field. This allowed them to offer insights and guidance to their fellow students, fostering a sense of camaraderie. They cherish the opportunity to share their experiences and knowledge, knowing they're helping shape the future IT professionals of tomorrow.

Influential Teachers and Professors:

In their academic journey, one professor stood out as an inspiration. Sir Michael Ferd played a pivotal role in motivating the graduate to pursue their four-year degree. His encouragement and belief in the greater opportunities that would come with a more extensive education ignited a sense of purpose. He instilled the importance of academic dedication and the value of a four-year degree, setting the graduate on their path to success.
